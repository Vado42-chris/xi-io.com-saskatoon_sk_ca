# Cultural Protocols

This project covers Saskatoon as a place with deep history, living communities, Indigenous presence, colonial records, civic institutions, neighbourhood memory, and contemporary public life.

## Core posture

The repository should not flatten local history into only municipal records or recent news. Deep place, Indigenous context, treaty context, Métis history, migration, displacement, neighbourhood change, and community memory must be handled as civic-memory records with source and uncertainty notes.

## Indigenous and cultural records

Records involving Indigenous history, ceremony, cultural knowledge, sacred places, burial places, residential schools, child welfare, trauma, or community-specific knowledge require heightened review.

Use `review_required` or `blocked` when public display could be harmful, extractive, inaccurate, or culturally inappropriate.

## Oral history and consent

Oral history entries require consent metadata before public display.

Minimum consent metadata:

- Contributor name or pseudonym preference
- Consent date
- Permission scope
- Revocation or correction path where practical
- Whether audio, transcript, summary, or metadata only may be public
- Sensitive topics flag

## Avoid extractive framing

Do not treat vulnerable communities as data objects. Records should preserve source, context, uncertainty, and dignity. Public outputs should avoid spectacle, ranking, and neighbourhood shaming.
